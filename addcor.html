Welcome to the MathFun Addition Tutorial!

Earlier, we learned that subtraction is when you take things away. Now, we’ll explore addition, which is when you give or add things!

Think about it like this: in the subtraction example, you were left with 3 slices of pizza. After finishing them, you’re still hungry, so you ask your mum for more, and she gives you 2 more slices. We can write this like:

3 + 2 = 5

It doesn’t matter in what order you add the numbers — you’ll still get the same result!

What is Addition?

Addition is easy because you're just adding more instead of taking away. But when the numbers get bigger, it can feel a bit tricky. That’s where a special trick called Breaking Apart Numbers comes in.

Breaking Apart Numbers

To make big additions easier, you can break the numbers into smaller parts (tens and ones), then add them separately. Let’s try it with this problem:

65 + 78 = ?

Start by adding the ones:
8 + 5 = 13

Now, add the tens:
60 + 70 = 130

Finally, add the two results together:
130 + 13 = 143

So, 65 + 78 = 143!
